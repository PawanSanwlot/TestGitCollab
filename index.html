About Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet in the universe that is inhabited by life. It is the largest of the terrestrial planets in the Solar System and the fifth largest overall. Earth is sometimes referred to as the "Blue Planet" because of its water-rich surface.

  • Mass: 5.972 × 10^24 kg
  • Radius: 6,371 km
  • Surface area: 510.1 million km²
  • Volume: 1.0832 × 10^12 km³
  • Density: 5.51 g/cm³
  • Gravity: 9.807 m/s²
  • Magnetic field: 0.5 gauss
  • Atmosphere: 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, 1% other gases
  • Hydrosphere: 71% water, 29% land
  • Biosphere: Supports a wide variety of life forms

Facts About Earth

Here are some interesting facts about Earth:

  • Earth is the only planet in the Solar System that has liquid water on its surface.
  • Earth is the only planet in the Solar System that has a magnetic field.
  • Earth is the only planet in the Solar System that has a moon.
  • Earth is the only planet in the Solar System that has a solid core.
  • Earth is the only planet in the Solar System that has a breathable atmosphere.
  • Earth is the only planet in the Solar System that has a biosphere.
  • Earth is the only planet in the Solar System that has a civilization.
